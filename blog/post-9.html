Legal Checklist for Short-Term Rentals in India

Running a short-term rental business in India is highly rewarding — but it comes with legal responsibilities that many property owners overlook. Non-compliance can result in fines, forced closures, or legal disputes that wipe out months of rental income. This checklist covers everything a Jaipur property owner needs to stay fully compliant while operating a successful short-term rental.

1. Police Verification & Guest Registration

Under Indian law, accommodation providers — including private short-term rental hosts — are required to maintain a register of guests and, in certain states, submit guest details to local police. In Rajasthan, this requirement is enforced particularly strictly. Every guest must complete a Form C (C-Form) with their identity details before or upon check-in.

2. GST Registration & Tax Compliance

If your annual rental income from short-term rentals exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states), GST registration is mandatory. The applicable GST rate for accommodation services depends on the declared tariff per night — properties charging above ₹7,500 per night attract 18% GST.

Even below the mandatory threshold, voluntary GST registration is advisable if you plan to scale your rental portfolio. It allows you to claim input tax credits on furnishing and maintenance expenses, reducing your overall tax burden.

3. Income Tax on Rental Income

Rental income from short-term lets is taxable under "Income from House Property" or "Income from Business" depending on the nature and scale of operations. Property owners are entitled to deductions including municipal taxes paid, standard deduction of 30% on net annual value, and interest on home loan if applicable.

We strongly recommend consulting a CA familiar with rental income taxation to optimise your tax structure from the beginning. Proper structuring can legally reduce your effective tax rate significantly.

"Legal compliance is not a burden — it is the foundation of a sustainable rental business. The owners who treat compliance seriously are the ones who scale without fear."

4. Rental Agreement & Property Documentation

While short-term rentals do not require a formal registered lease agreement (unlike long-term rentals), it is essential to have a clear terms-and-conditions document that guests agree to before booking. This should cover house rules, damage liability, cancellation policy, and prohibited activities.

Additionally, ensure your property's title documents, electricity connection, and municipal tax records are in order. Properties with disputed ownership or pending dues are legally vulnerable and can be flagged by local authorities during inspections.

5. Housing Society & Building Regulations

Many residential apartment complexes in Jaipur have Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs) or housing society rules that restrict or regulate short-term rentals. Before listing your property, review your society's bylaws and, if necessary, obtain written permission from the society management.
